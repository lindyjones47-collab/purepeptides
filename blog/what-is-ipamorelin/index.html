What Is Ipamorelin? A Plain-English Research Overview

Published 13 June 2026 · PurePeptides Research Hub · For research use only.

Ipamorelin is one of the most-searched research peptides, so it is worth a plain-English overview. This article is a quick introduction; for the full technical reference — molecular weight, sequence, CAS number and the published literature — see our in-depth ipamorelin research guide. Everything here is for laboratory research use only.

What is ipamorelin, in simple terms?

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide — a short chain of five amino acids (sequence Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2). In the research literature it is described as a selective ligand of the ghrelin / growth-hormone-secretagogue (GHS) receptor. It is supplied as a lyophilised (freeze-dried) powder and reconstituted in the laboratory before use.

Ipamorelin at a glance

  • Class: synthetic pentapeptide (5 amino acids)
  • Sequence: Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2
  • Receptor (research): ghrelin / growth-hormone-secretagogue (GHS) receptor
  • Molecular weight: approximately 711.9 g/mol
  • CAS number: 170851-70-4
  • Form: lyophilised powder, >99% purity — research use only

What makes ipamorelin distinctive?

What sets ipamorelin apart in the literature is its reported selectivity. Its original characterisation described it acting on the growth-hormone axis in preclinical models with minimal reported effect on other hormones such as ACTH and cortisol, relative to earlier growth-hormone-releasing peptides (GHRPs). That is one reason it is frequently chosen as a reference compound in growth-hormone-secretagogue research. These are observations from experimental systems and are not claims about any effect in humans or animals.

Ipamorelin and related peptides

Researchers often place ipamorelin next to other growth-hormone-axis peptides. It acts on the ghrelin / GHS receptor, which distinguishes it from the GHRH-receptor analogues such as sermorelin and CJC-1295. For the head-to-head detail, see our ipamorelin vs sermorelin comparison.

How ipamorelin is handled in research

Lyophilised ipamorelin is typically stored at -20°C, protected from light and moisture. In the laboratory it is commonly reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, then kept refrigerated at 2-8°C with freeze-thaw cycles minimised.
