Buying Ipamorelin in the UK: A Research Buyer's Guide

Published 13 June 2026 · PurePeptides Research Hub · For research use only.

Ipamorelin is one of the most-searched research peptides, and UK laboratories tend to ask the same practical questions before sourcing it: what exactly is it, how does it differ from sermorelin, and how do you buy research-grade material you can trust? What is ipamorelin?

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide (five amino acids; sequence Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2) described in the research literature as a selective ligand of the ghrelin / growth-hormone-secretagogue (GHS) receptor. It is supplied as a lyophilised (freeze-dried) powder and reconstituted in the laboratory before use. For the full chemical profile, see our in-depth ipamorelin research guide.

Ipamorelin vs sermorelin

The comparison UK researchers ask about most is ipamorelin vs sermorelin, and the short answer is that they act on different receptors. Sermorelin is a 29-amino-acid analogue of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH 1-29) that targets the GHRH receptor, whereas ipamorelin is a much smaller five-amino-acid ligand of the ghrelin / GHS receptor. We break this down with a side-by-side table in our ipamorelin vs sermorelin comparison.

What to look for when you buy ipamorelin

Whether you buy ipamorelin in the UK or elsewhere, the factors that matter for reproducible research are the same:

  • Purity — research-grade ipamorelin should be supplied at greater than 99% purity, since synthesis by-products can confound results.
  • Certificate of analysis (COA) — a reputable supplier can provide a batch COA showing the HPLC purity figure and mass-spectrometry identity confirmation.
  • Correct labelling — material should be clearly labelled for research use only, not for human or animal consumption.
  • A traceable supplier — a registered company with a real address and a support channel, rather than an anonymous marketplace listing.

Storage and reconstitution

Lyophilised ipamorelin is typically stored at -20°C, protected from light and moisture. In the laboratory it is commonly reconstituted with bacteriostatic water; once in solution it is kept refrigerated at 2-8°C with freeze-thaw cycles minimised.
