MOTS-c Stability: How Long After Reconstitution?

Published 28 February 2026 · PurePeptides Research Hub · For research use only.

A common question in peptide research is how long MOTS-c remains usable after reconstitution. There is no single universal figure — stability in solution depends on storage conditions rather than the calendar alone. This is a general research overview, for laboratory use only.

What affects peptide stability in solution?

Once a lyophilised peptide is reconstituted, several factors govern how quickly it degrades:

  • Temperature — refrigeration at 2-8°C slows degradation; room temperature accelerates it.
  • Freeze-thaw cycles — repeated freezing and thawing is one of the most damaging things for a peptide in solution.
  • Light and oxygen — exposure to light and air can promote breakdown, so amber or shielded storage helps.
  • Solvent — bacteriostatic water limits microbial growth in multi-use vials compared with plain sterile water.

Practical takeaway

In research handling, reconstituted peptides are generally kept refrigerated, protected from light, and used within a planned window rather than stored indefinitely. Aliquoting before freezing is one way to avoid repeated freeze-thaw cycles. Our reconstitution calculator can help plan aliquot volumes.

Frequently asked questions

How long is MOTS-c stable after reconstitution? ⌄

There is no single fixed figure; stability in solution depends on storage. Refrigeration at 2-8°C, protection from light, and minimising freeze-thaw cycles all extend usable life.

What damages reconstituted peptides most? ⌄

Repeated freeze-thaw cycles, warmth, and exposure to light and air. Aliquoting before freezing helps avoid repeated thawing of the whole batch.
