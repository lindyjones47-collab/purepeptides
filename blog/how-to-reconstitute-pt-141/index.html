How to Reconstitute PT-141 for Research

Published 5 May 2026 · PurePeptides Research Hub · For research use only.

PT-141 (Bremelanotide) is supplied as a lyophilised powder and reconstituted in the laboratory before use. This is a general research-handling overview; for the compound's chemistry see our PT-141 research guide. Everything here is for laboratory research use only.

1. Choose a solvent

PT-141 is water-soluble, and bacteriostatic water is the most commonly cited reconstitution solvent for research handling of multi-use vials.

2. Add the solvent gently

Run the solvent slowly down the inner wall of the vial onto the powder, then let it dissolve without vigorous shaking. A gentle swirl is preferable to agitation.

3. Work out the concentration

Concentration is the vial's peptide mass divided by the solvent volume. For instance, 10 mg in 2 mL gives 5 mg/mL. Use our reconstitution calculator to plan volumes precisely.

4. Store the solution

Keep the lyophilised vial at -20°C until use. Once in solution, store refrigerated at 2-8°C and minimise freeze-thaw cycles.

What is used to reconstitute PT-141? ⌄

PT-141 is water-soluble and is commonly reconstituted with bacteriostatic water for research handling.

How should reconstituted PT-141 be stored? ⌄

Refrigerated at 2-8°C with freeze-thaw cycles minimised; the lyophilised vial is stored at -20°C until use.
