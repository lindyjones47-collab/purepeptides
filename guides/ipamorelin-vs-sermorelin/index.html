What is Ipamorelin?

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide (five amino acids) with the sequence Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2. In research literature it is described as a selective ghrelin / growth-hormone-secretagogue receptor ligand. It is supplied as a lyophilised powder for laboratory research.

What is Sermorelin?

Sermorelin is a synthetic peptide corresponding to the first 29 amino acids of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH 1-29), the N-terminal fragment of GHRH. It is supplied as a lyophilised powder for laboratory research.

Read more in our Sermorelin research guide, or view the Sermorelin product page.

Key differences

The defining difference is receptor class. Sermorelin is a GHRH-receptor analogue — the 29-amino-acid N-terminal fragment (GHRH 1-29) of growth hormone-releasing hormone. Ipamorelin is a much smaller five-amino-acid ligand of the ghrelin / growth-hormone-secretagogue (GHS) receptor. They engage two different receptors within the growth-hormone axis and differ greatly in size (29 vs 5 residues) and molecular weight (~3358 vs ~711.9 g/mol).

How researchers distinguish Ipamorelin and Sermorelin

In research the two are often studied side by side precisely because they represent the two principal receptor families in this area: the GHRH receptor and the ghrelin / GHS receptor. A study's choice between them is an experimental-design decision driven by which receptor pathway is under investigation — it is not a question of relative potency for any applied or human purpose, and nothing here should be read as such.

Frequently asked questions

Are ipamorelin and sermorelin the same? ⌄

No. They are chemically distinct peptides that act on different receptors: sermorelin is a 29-amino-acid GHRH-receptor analogue, while ipamorelin is a 5-amino-acid ghrelin / GHS-receptor ligand.

What is the main difference between ipamorelin and sermorelin? ⌄

Receptor class and size. Sermorelin targets the GHRH receptor and is 29 amino acids; ipamorelin targets the ghrelin / GHS receptor and is 5 amino acids.

Are ipamorelin and sermorelin studied together in research? ⌄

Yes &mdash; in growth-hormone-axis research they are sometimes used together as representatives of the two main receptor families (GHRH vs GHS). This is an experimental-design choice, not guidance for any human use.
